Atal Bihari Vajpayee

1924 - 2018

10th Prime Minister of India

Atal Bihari Vajpayee (25 December 1924 â€“ 16 August 2018) was an Indian politician and statesman[1] who served three terms as the 10th Prime Minister of India, first for a term of 13 days in 1996, then for a period of 13 months from 1998 to 1999, followed by a full term from 1999 to 2004. Vajpayee was one of the co-founders and a senior leader of the BJP. He was a member of the RSS, a Hindu nationalist volunteer organisation. He was the first Indian prime minister not of the INC to serve a full term in office. He was also a renowned poet and a writer.

He was a member of the Indian Parliament for over five decades, having been elected ten times to the Lok Sabha, the lower house, and twice to the Rajya Sabha, the upper house. He served as the MP (LS) for Lucknow, retiring from active politics in 2009 due to health concerns. He was among the founding members of the BJS, of which he was president from 1968 to 1972. The BJS merged with several other parties to form the JP, which won the 1977 general election. In March 1977, Vajpayee became the Minister of External Affairs in the cabinet of Prime Minister Morarji Desai. He resigned in 1979, and the Janata alliance collapsed soon after. Former members of the BJS formed the BJP in 1980, with Vajpayee its first president.

Biographies

  • Early Life: Atal Bihari Vajpayee was born on December 25, 1924, in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh, India, to Krishna Bihari Vajpayee and Krishna Devi. He completed his education in Gwalior and later pursued higher studies in Delhi.
  • Founding Member of the BJP: Vajpayee was one of the founding members of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in 1980. He played a crucial role in shaping the party's ideology and political vision.
  • Member of Parliament: Vajpayee was elected as a Member of Parliament several times from various constituencies. He represented Lucknow in the Lok Sabha and also served as a Member of Rajya Sabha.
  • Prime Minister of India: Vajpayee served as the Prime Minister of India three times. He first assumed office in 1996, for a brief period of 13 days, followed by two terms from 1998 to 2004.
  • Pokhran Nuclear Tests: During his tenure as Prime Minister, Vajpayee ordered a series of nuclear tests in Pokhran, Rajasthan, in May 1998. This move demonstrated India's nuclear capability and solidified its position on the global stage.
  • Economic Reforms: Vajpayee's government initiated various economic reforms to boost India's economy and attract foreign investment. His policies laid the foundation for the country's economic growth in the subsequent years.
  • Initiatives for Peace: Vajpayee was known for his efforts towards peace and diplomacy. He extended the hand of friendship towards Pakistan and visited the country in a historic bus journey in 1999.
  • A Poet and Writer: Apart from his political pursuits, Vajpayee was also an accomplished poet and writer. He authored several books and poetry collections, showcasing his literary talents.
  • Padma Vibhushan Awardee: In recognition of his significant contributions to Indian politics and society, Atal Bihari Vajpayee was honored with India's second-highest civilian award, the Padma Vibhushan, in 1992.
